About GoSeva


Cow is a very important animal. One Hindu goddess, Bhoomi (भुमि), is usually shown in the form of a cow. She represents the Earth. In the Vedas, the oldest of the Hindu scriptures, the cow is associated with Aditi, the mother of all the gods. Every body part of a cow symbolizes some venerable aspect as detailed below: • Legs symbolize four Vedas (Rig, Yajur, Sama and Atharvana), • Teats symbolize four puruṣārthas (human pursuits) namely Dharma (righteousness), Artha (material wealth), Kama(desire) and Moksha (freedom). • Horns symbolize the Gods, • Face symbolizes the Sun and Moon, • Shoulders symbolize Agni devatā, the god of fire. Garuda Purana, Upanishads, Jyothisha Shastra, Brahmanda Purana and many scriptures have stressed on the many benefits of GoSeva (Serving the Cow). The cow is considered the most holy animal as it has all the 365 Gods, 9 Grahas, 12 Rashis & 27 Nakshatras residing in it. Even Lord Sri Krishna has described GoSeva in great detail in Brindavana. Scriptures also mention that Go Daana (donating a cow) is equivalent to performing an Ashwamedha Yagna. We all celebrate our birthdays and anniversaries in a grand way by inviting many guests but are not sure how many of these guests will actually bless you. Most of the times these guests will discuss how bad the food at your party was. Instead by doing GoSeva on these special days in your life you avail many benefits and your prayers read God directly and you receive divine blessings. It is proven that Cow’s Urine & Cowdung has steroids and antibodies. People in olden days already knew this fact and have used this extensively to maintain health, keep their homes free of pests and this was also used as a great fertilizer. Go Seva is best when done in the morning during Sunrise. However it may be done before Sunset as well.

Type of Goseva

Go Daana

The Garuda Purana indicates that after death a cow needs to be donated on Vaikunta Samaradhana (11th Day after death). There are 16 cities that one has to pass on the way to Yama’s abode. They are Yamya, Souri, Nagendra, Gandharva, Shailagama, Krouncha, Krura, Vichitra, Vahvapada, Duhkada, Nankranda, Sutapta, Roudra, Payovarshana, Shitadhya & Vahubhiti. In between the cities named Vichitra & Vahvapada, the river Vaitarani which is a terrible river made of blood and guarded by fierce flesh eating birds has to be crossed. By donating a cow a boat will be provided to safely cross this river. In case a person has no children or if there is any uncertainty if Go Daana will be done by family members a person can donate a cow during his/her lifetime itself. There is no need for family members to do any ceremonies. The method specified in the scriptures is the Cowherd (Go Paalaka) should hold the tip of the cows tail and the person making the donation will need to keep a thulasi leaf on the hand holding the tail and pour a Udharani of water on the thulasi leaf.

Go Dhaana seva starting from Rs 50000/-

Go Bhojana

Feeding a cow as prescribed by the vet 3 times is one of the Go Sevas. It is recommended that you personally feed a cow with fruits & fodder. However if one cannot do Go Bhojana in person the expense incurred to feed a cow may be paid to the Go Paalaka. Childless couples desirous of having a child may feed a cow and its calf or just the calf as well.

Go Bhojana Seva starting from Rs 150/-(1 Day Meals per Cow)

Go Paalana Seva

Bathing, Cleaning the cowshed, Removing Cowdung and maintaining hygiene, Brushing their coats, Caring for them and taking them for grazing under supervision are some of the Go Paalana Sevas.

About Datta Dhaama Goshala

Datta Dhaama is an ashram which is a sprawling 8 acre piece of land that was inhabited by the Pandavas during their exile and sages like Garageshwara Muni who did their penance here. Since this land was inhabited by the Pandavas and sages, Datta Dhaama exudes an atmosphere of peace, tranquility and spiritual bliss. Datta Dhaama is managed by Sri Shirdi Sai Biksha Kendra, a social service organisation under the divine guidance and leadership of "Mudra Ratna" "Mudra Brahma" Dr. Sri Lakshmi Srinivas Guruji. A fully functional cowshed (Goshala) with cows and required equipment is available at Datta Dhaama. Taking Sri Guruji’s gracious advice many people have been rendering different kinds of service to the cows (Go Seva) to get rid of their problems & illnesses with great results and success. Datta Dhaama is located at Garageshwarapura, Guddemarenahalli, Magadi Taluk, Ramanagar District, Karnataka, India.
